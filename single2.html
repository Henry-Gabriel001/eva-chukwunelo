Equal Right

Is the law guilding human right still working?

MAY 30, 2025 Eva Chukwunelo

Human rights are rights we have simply because we exist as human beings - they are not granted by any state. These universal rights are inherent to us all, regardless of nationality, sex, national or ethnic origin, color, religion, language, or any other status. They range from the most fundamental - the right to life - to those that make life worth living, such as the rights to food, education, work, health, and liberty.

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), adopted by the UN General Assembly in 1948, was the first legal document to set out the fundamental human rights to be universally protected. The UDHR, which turns 75 on 10 December 2023, continues to be the foundation of all international human rights law. Its 30 articles provide the principles and building blocks of current and future human rights conventions, treaties and other legal instruments. The UDHR, together with the 2 covenants - the International Covenant for Civil and Political Rights, and the International Covenant for Economic, Social and Cultural Rights - make up the International Bill of Rights.

What Are Equal Rights? Equal rights mean that every person is entitled to the same legal rights and opportunities without discrimination. These rights include access to education, employment, healthcare, voting, and protection under the law. They form the foundation of justice and fairness in any society. The Significance of Equal Rights Promotes Justice: Equal rights ensure that everyone receives fair treatment and justice, promoting social harmony. Reduces Discrimination: Upholding equal rights combats prejudice and bias, fostering inclusiveness. Encourages Economic Growth: When all individuals have equal access to opportunities, societies benefit from diverse talents and innovations. Enhances Social Stability: Fair treatment builds trust in institutions and reduces social conflicts. Challenges to Achieving Equal Rights Despite global efforts, many barriers still exist: Gender Inequality: Women and girls often face unequal treatment in many areas, including education, employment, and legal rights. Racial and Ethnic Discrimination: Minority groups often encounter prejudice, violence, and unequal access to services. Economic Disparities: Poverty can limit access to essential rights and opportunities. Legal and Cultural Barriers: Some laws and societal norms maintain inequality and restrict freedoms. Moving Towards Equality Advancing equal rights requires a multifaceted approach: Legal Reforms: Enacting and enforcing laws that protect against discrimination. Education: Raising awareness about rights and promoting inclusive attitudes. Empowerment: Supporting marginalized groups to claim their rights and participate fully in society. Global Cooperation: Encouraging countries and organizations to work together toward equality standards. Conclusion Equal rights are the cornerstone of a fair and progressive society. When everyone is treated with dignity and respect, communities become stronger, more vibrant, and more resilient. While progress has been made, ongoing efforts are necessary to eliminate inequalities and build a world where true fairness and justice prevail for all.
